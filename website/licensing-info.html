Compliance Guide

What You Can Do

Read License Terms

Always read and understand the specific license terms before using music.

Provide Attribution

Credit artists when required by the license terms.

Keep Records

Maintain documentation of your music licenses and usage.

Respect Usage Limits

Stay within the scope of your license agreement.

What to Avoid

Ignore License Terms

Don't use music without understanding the license requirements.

Skip Attribution

Don't forget to credit artists when required.

Exceed Usage Rights

Don't use music beyond what your license allows.

Assume All Rights

Don't assume you have unlimited rights to modify or distribute.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between royalty-free and Creative Commons?

Royalty-free music requires a one-time payment but allows unlimited use without ongoing fees. Creative Commons music is free but may have specific usage conditions and attribution requirements.

Do I need to credit the artist?

It depends on the license type. Royalty-free licenses typically don't require attribution, while Creative Commons licenses usually do. Always check the specific license terms.

Can I use the same track in multiple projects?

Most royalty-free licenses allow unlimited use across multiple projects. Creative Commons licenses may have restrictions. Check your specific license for details.

What if I need to modify the music?

Modification rights vary by license. Royalty-free licenses usually allow modifications, while Creative Commons licenses may have specific conditions. Always verify before making changes.

Is the license transferable?

Most licenses are non-transferable and tied to the original purchaser. If you're working for a client, they may need their own license.
